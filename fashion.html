The Fashion Industry and Sustainability

The fashion industry has a sustainability problem. The seasonal and deposable nature of the way we consume clothes and fashion is one of the biggest issues to tackle in trying to save the earth. Fashion is designed to be fleeting and constantly new, which is portrayed out in the fashion seasons and the always-changing trends in the industry. The rise of fast fashion in the past 5 years is a prime example of issue we are facing.

Fast fashion creates cheap, low-quality clothes to be worn for a few months and then disposed of quickly. Brands, such as PrettyLittleThing, Misguided and Boohoo, have founded their businesses on the fast fashion cycle and will probably not survive the move away from fast fashion that is needed. On the other hand, the popularity of second hand and vintage shopping is growing massively. Through the classic charity shop and online communities, such as Depop and Vinted, second clothing is becoming increasingly assessable as the salve for fast fashion. We, as consumers, as well as the fashion industry as a whole, need to commit to buying sustainable, high-quality clothes that will last, to begin fixing fashionâ€™s sustainability problem.
