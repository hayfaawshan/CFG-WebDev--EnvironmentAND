Youth For Climate Action

It is undeniable that climate change will affect today’s youth the most and so they are taking a stand and demanding world leaders to take more urgent action, before it is too late. They are not only making sure their voices are heard, but making sure they are the voices of the most vulnerable communities.

Here are some of the youth leading the movement and conversations.

Greta Thunberg - Sweden

Greta is a household name spearheading young climate activism through her powerful speeches and protests, inspiring a whole generation of youth climate change activists.

“Yes, we are failing, but there is still time to turn everything around -- we can still fix this.”

Follow their journey!

Twitter Instagram

Bruno Rodriguez - Argentina

Bruno was invited to speak at the first UN Youth Climate Summit in 2019 and powerfully declared climate change as the “political, economic and cultural crisis of our time.” He campaigns for lower carbon emissions in his native Argentina.

“Our world leaders have the obligation to make radical change. But change rarely takes place from the top down. It happens when millions of people demand change”

Follow their journey!

Instagram

Vanessa Nakate - Uganda

Vanessa is the founder of the Youth for Future Africa and the Rise Up Movement. She highlights the lack of diversity and representation in the environmental movement, particularly since African nations are some of the most vulnerable to and least prepared for climate change.

“Nature is not a place to visit, it’s home, preserve it. Stop denying the Earth is dying”

Follow their journey!

Twitter Instagram

Isra Hirsi -
USA

Co-founder and co-executive of US Youth Climate Strike, a student-led movement of youth who are demanding action by organising rallies. Isra brings attention to the fact that people of colour disproportionately are affected by environmental issues.

"The older generations need to be allies, they’re more wise. I think it’s really important for them to work towards supporting students, helping to make climate change more accessible for young people”

Follow their journey!

Twitter Instagram

John Paul Jose -
India

John is leading the conversation on climate change in India, and how it directly affects the Indian people. He is the organiser of Fridays for the Future India and is championing the all important discussion with India’s political leaders.

“India should declare a climate emergency. If we don’t act now, we will be passing on a dying planet to the next generation."

Follow their journey!

Twitter Instagram

Xiye Bastida -
Mexico/USA

Xiye had to flee her native country of Mexico and relocate to NYC as floods meant she could no longer attend school. She is now the organiser of Fridays for the Future NYC and the US Global Climate Strike in NYC. She is a strong voice who is keeping the perspectives of indigineous people in the climate conversation.

“We don’t want people to suffer from the climate crisis to realize we are in a crisis”

Follow their journey!
